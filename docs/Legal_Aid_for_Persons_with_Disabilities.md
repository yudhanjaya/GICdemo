The Legal Aid Commission has established the Disabled Persons Legal Aid Desk to ensure the rights of persons with disabilities are protected and promoted. This service provides free legal advice and assistance to destitute persons with disabilities on issues such as maintenance, accident claims, loss of property, and fundamental rights.

The Disabled Persons Desk aims to:

- Help persons with disabilities claim the rights guaranteed to them under the Protection of Disabled Persons Rights Act No. 28 of 1996.
- Raise awareness about the rights of persons with disabilities among the community and school children.
- Collaborate with the National Secretariat for Disabled Persons to empower persons with disabilities.

The Disabled Persons Desk, led by Attorney-at-Law Kalani Medagoda, conducts various programs to promote awareness and prevent discrimination against persons with disabilities. This includes publishing a booklet on the rights of persons with disabilities and organizing special events to mark the International Day of Persons with Disabilities.

If you need legal assistance or have any questions about the rights of persons with disabilities, you can:

- Call the Disabled Persons Desk at +94 11 2 429 429
    - Lines are open from 8:30 AM to 4:30 PM, Monday to Friday
    - You can speak in Sinhala, Tamil, or English
    - Calls are charged at normal rates
- Visit any Divisional Secretariat office
    - Bring your National Identity Card
    - Staff can explain the process in person
    - No appointment needed
- Email disableddesk@legalaid.gov.lk
    - Responses usually take 2 working days
    - Include your phone number in your email
